# Product Marketing Context

*Last updated: 2026-04-16*

## Product Overview
**One-liner:** Custom software development agency that builds mobile apps, web platforms, and business dashboards for companies that have outgrown off-the-shelf tools.
**What it does:** Unitaspro designs and builds custom digital products — mobile apps (iOS & Android), web applications, SaaS platforms, admin dashboards, and booking systems — on a fixed-price, fixed-timeline basis. We take businesses from idea to production-ready product in 4-12 weeks.
**Product category:** Custom Software Development / IT Services Agency
**Product type:** Service (project-based, fixed-price engagements)
**Business model:** Fixed-price project delivery. Scoping call > quote > build > deliver. No hourly billing. Ongoing maintenance available post-launch.

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** Businesses run critical operations on spreadsheets, WhatsApp groups, and generic SaaS tools that don't fit their workflow — causing inefficiency, errors, and inability to scale.
**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Off-the-shelf SaaS: too generic, can't customize for specific workflows, monthly fees add up
- Freelancers: unreliable delivery, no architecture thinking, disappear after launch
- Big agencies: 6-month timelines, $200K+ budgets, overbuild everything
- In-house team: expensive to hire, slow to ramp up, hard to find good talent
**What it costs them:** Hours of manual work daily, lost customers from poor digital experience, missed revenue from inability to scale operations, competitive disadvantage.
**Emotional tension:** "Am I going to waste $50K on something that doesn't work?" / "Every month we delay, we're losing ground to competitors who already have apps."

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct:** Other custom dev agencies (Toptal, local agencies, offshore dev shops) — fall short because they overbuild, under-communicate, or deliver code that needs rewriting within a year.
**Secondary:** No-code/low-code platforms (Bubble, Adalo, FlutterFlow) — fall short because they hit walls at scale, can't handle complex logic, and you don't own the infrastructure.
**Indirect:** Hiring in-house developers — falls short because it takes 3-6 months to hire, costs 3-5x more annually, and you still need architecture guidance.

- Industry-specific expertise — we've built for transport, healthcare, finance, etc.

## Switching Dynamics
**Push:** Current tools are costing them time, customers, and money. Manual processes that worked for 10 clients break at 100.
**Pull:** A single platform built around their exact workflow. Fixed price so they can budget. Fast delivery so they can compete.
**Habit:** "We've always done it this way." Familiarity with spreadsheets and existing tools. Team resistance to change.
**Anxiety:** "What if it doesn't work?" / "What if they disappear after taking the money?" / "What if it takes twice as long?"
